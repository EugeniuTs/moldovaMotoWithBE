# MoldovaMoto Brand Voice

A short reference for anyone writing copy for the site, email, social, or sales collateral. Read before you write; paste new copy into the checklist at the bottom before shipping.

---

## Who we sound like

**The knowledgeable rider-friend.** Someone who lives here, rides what they sell, knows the back roads, speaks plainly, respects your time, and isn't trying to upsell you. Not a tour-desk brochure. Not a motorcycle influencer.

If the brand were a person: mid-40s, rides 20,000 km a year, has done the Alps and the Balkans and decided Moldova was better, speaks three languages fluently, will tell you exactly which café has the good plăcintă and which one doesn't.

---

## Audience

- **Primary:** experienced motorcyclists, 30–60, from Germany, Austria, Switzerland, UK, US, Netherlands, Scandinavia. Already ridden the Alps, the Pyrenees, maybe the Balkans. Looking for somewhere new without the coach tours.
- **Secondary:** moto-curious travellers on a first guided trip — need reassurance that Moldova is safe and that the operator knows what they're doing.
- **Never patronise either group.** The first knows bikes; don't explain what ABS is. The second knows travel; don't oversell "adventure".

---

## Voice attributes

### Confident
- **We are:** direct, specific, willing to make claims and own them.
- **We are not:** hedging, cautious, academic.
- **Sounds like:** "The canonical Moldova ride."
- **Not:** "Arguably one of the best tours we offer."

### Specific
- **We are:** concrete numbers, real places, named things.
- **We are not:** generic marketing adjectives ("luxury", "world-class", "unforgettable").
- **Sounds like:** "€5 Uber from the airport, 25 minutes." / "CFMOTO 800MT, 95 hp, 19-inch front wheel."
- **Not:** "Affordable transport available." / "Premium adventure motorcycles."

### Insider
- **We are:** local knowledge, named villages, opinions about which road to take.
- **We are not:** guidebook-generic, tourist-board bland.
- **Sounds like:** "We skip the R-14 and thread through Peresecina and Curchi."
- **Not:** "Scenic routes through Moldova's countryside."

### Warm, not chummy
- **We are:** welcoming, first-person plural ("we ride"), small moments of humour.
- **We are not:** casual to the point of unprofessional, emoji-heavy, exclamation-prone.
- **Sounds like:** "Beers you'll buy the guide: budget €15."
- **Not:** "You're gonna LOVE this tour 🏍️🔥"

## Tone spectrum

The **voice** stays constant. The **tone** dials up or down depending on context.

| Surface              | Boldness (1–10) | Warmth (1–10) | Notes |
|----------------------|:---------------:|:-------------:|-------|
| Hero / headline CTA  | 9               | 5             | Allowed to shout. "Escape the Ordinary." "The Road Is Waiting." |
| Tour cards           | 7               | 6             | Confident claims, friendly framing |
| Blog pillar pages    | 5               | 7             | Measured authority, insider details |
| FAQ                  | 3               | 7             | Patient, direct, no marketing |
| Error messages       | 2               | 9             | Empathetic, blameless, helpful |
| Booking confirmation | 4               | 8             | Reassuring, specific, action-oriented |
| Incident / outage    | 1               | 9             | Accountable, human, no spin |
| Legal / terms        | 1               | 3             | Formal, precise, no voice flourishes |

## Style rules

### Grammar & punctuation
- **Oxford comma:** yes. Always. ("helmet, jacket, and gloves")
- **Contractions:** use them. "we're", "don't", "it's".
- **Em dash:** spaced ` — `, not `—` or `--`.
- **Exclamation marks:** one per page maximum, ideally zero. Let the specifics land the emphasis.
- **Ellipsis:** avoid. If you need a dramatic pause, rewrite the sentence.
- **ALL CAPS:** never for emphasis. Use bold. Fine for section labels/kickers (uppercase letter-spaced).

### Numbers & units
- **Spell out** one through nine in prose; **numerals** for 10+.
- **Prices:** €220, €1,050 (thousands separator is a comma).
- **Distances:** metric first (km), imperial in parentheses only if relevant to US readers.
- **Temperatures:** °C.
- **Times:** 24-hour in itineraries ("09:30 — out of the city"), 12-hour in prose where it reads natural.

### Formatting
- **Headings:** sentence case. "How to get there", not "How To Get There".
- **Link text:** descriptive. "See the 3-Day Adventure →", never "Click here".
- **Lists:** use them when there are 3+ parallel items. Don't force bullets onto prose.
- **Bold:** for emphasis inside prose. Not for decoration.
- **Italic:** for titles of things, foreign phrases ("plăcintă"), or the occasional emphatic word. Not for quotes — use quotation marks.

## Terminology

### Places and proper nouns (use these exactly)

| Use                       | Not                              | Why |
|---------------------------|----------------------------------|-----|
| Chișinău                  | Chisinau, Kishinev               | Local, current spelling with diacritics |
| Moldova (EN)              | Republic of Moldova              | Shorter, more recognisable |
| Moldawien (DE)            | Moldau                           | "Moldau" means the Vltava river. Use only in the phrase "Republik Moldau" where the official name is required |
| Orheiul Vechi             | Old Orhei, Orheiul-Vechi         | Local name; *"Old Orhei"* OK on first mention in parentheses |
| Nistru                    | Dniester                         | Use the local name |
| Transnistria              | Transdniestria, PMR              | Most common English form |
| CFMOTO 800MT              | CF Moto 800 MT, CFMoto 800MT     | Uppercase, no space |

### Product and tour names

| Use                           | Not                               |
|-------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| 1-Day Wine Ride               | One-day wine tour, Wine Ride 1-day |
| 3-Day Moldova Adventure       | Three-day adventure, 3-day tour   |
| 5-Day Grand Moldova Tour      | Grand Tour, 5-day Moldova         |
| the CFMOTO 800MT              | a CFMOTO, the CFMOTO bike          |

### Preferred words

| Use              | Not                            |
|------------------|--------------------------------|
| ride (verb)      | go / drive                     |
| rider            | customer / client              |
| departure        | trip date                      |
| guesthouse       | hotel / lodging                |
| winery           | wine producer / vineyard (unless literal) |
| itinerary        | schedule / programme           |

### Avoid

- **Superlatives without evidence** — "best", "only", "fastest", "world-class". If you must use one, attribute or quantify.
- **Claims of absolute safety** — "never had a safety incident", "zero risk", "100% safe". Say "operated without serious incident", "carefully managed", or similar.
- **Comparative disparagement of competitors** — reference tourist coaches, guided bus tours, or "the usual operators" instead of naming.
- **Ableist filler** — "crazy", "insane", "lame" as intensifiers.
- **"Easy"** — what's easy varies by person. Prefer "straightforward", "manageable", or describe the specifics ("flat tarmac, moderate pace").

## Legal & compliance

Before shipping any customer-facing copy, check:

- [ ] No absolute safety claims.
- [ ] No unsubstantiated superlatives ("best", "fastest", "only in the world").
- [ ] Comparative claims are fair and factual.
- [ ] No advice that creates liability if followed (e.g. "leave valuables in the panniers").
- [ ] Prices match the live offer schema (WineRide.jsx, ThreeDayAdventure.jsx, FiveDayGrand.jsx).
- [ ] Named individuals have given permission. No name-drops of controversial public figures.
- [ ] Testimonials have attribution.
- [ ] Cancellation, insurance, and license requirements match [Faq.jsx](src/pages/Faq.jsx).

## Pre-publish checklist

Paste this into every PR that touches copy:

- [ ] Read aloud — does it sound like the knowledgeable rider-friend?
- [ ] Removed every generic adjective that could be deleted without losing meaning.
- [ ] Every claim is specific, verifiable, or clearly positioned as opinion.
- [ ] Oxford comma everywhere.
- [ ] No exclamation marks unless truly earned.
- [ ] Place names use the table above.
- [ ] Links to other pages are real routes, not `#` placeholders or anchor guesses.
- [ ] CTA verb is active ("Reserve", "Book", "See") not passive ("Learn more").
- [ ] Tone dial matches the surface (see spectrum table).
- [ ] Pricing and dates match the tour pages.
- [ ] Legal checklist above is satisfied.

## Examples

### Good

> "We skip the R-14 and thread through Peresecina, Curchi, and Butuceni. Twice the twisties, almost zero traffic, and you get to approach Orheiul Vechi from the canyon side — the dramatic approach."

Why: named villages, specific claim ("twice the twisties"), opinion owned ("the dramatic approach").

### Bad

> "We take scenic back roads through charming Moldovan villages to deliver an unforgettable experience."

Why: generic ("scenic", "charming", "unforgettable"), no named places, no opinion, no differentiator.

### Good

> "Most visitors see the cave and leave. Don't. Ride 3 km further down the valley through Butuceni."

Why: direct imperative, specific distance, insider move.

### Bad

> "Don't miss the chance to explore the surrounding area — there's much more to see nearby!"

Why: soft imperative, vague, exclamation mark, marketing voice.
